"A common concern every year is the cost of fees. The fees for both the Open and Club divisions are comparable to other clubs. Fees are raised to keep pace with our court costs, tournament fees, uniforms and coach retention. We are a non-profit and only raise fees to the amounts needed to run our programs.

One of our hallmarks at Pumas are the six extra practice dates that almost no other club has because they hold practice on Sunday (These practices are missed due to Sunday NERVA tournaments and Easter) and Pumas practice on Saturday. Starting this year, we have approximately 42 hours of additional practice with the addition of a second practice (Tuesday) for all teams. In addition, all local multi-day tournaments (Winterfest, Qualifiers, Boston, Rhode Island Rumble) are also included in the season fee.

We hope you will stay with Pumas as we continue to improve our programs and give your daughters an experience they will both enjoy and benefit from."

Stephen Boyle, 2019 Club Director

Refund Policy

  • The tryout fee is non-refundable, even if the athlete does not attend any tryout.
  • The $150.00 season deposit is refundable if a girl is cut or drops by notifying the CLUB DIRECTOR by letter, email, or phone on or before November 23th, 2018 (Open or Club). Only refunds for medical necessity with a doctor's note will be allowed after these dates.
  • Both player and parent should know before the second tryout whether they want to be considered for placement on a Pumas team. You will lose your deposit if you drop after 11/23 for any reason other than medical.
  • The season deposit is holding a place for the player. Any player placed on a team but not accepting the position will lose their deposit.
  • If a player incurs a season-ending injury (with a doctor's note) before March 1st, 2019, a partial pro-rated refund will be issued. After March 1st, 2019, no refunds will be given.
  • No refund will be given to a player who pays the Open team fee but whose team plays in the Club division because they are still practicing as an Open team.
  • Refunds are not given for an athletes' inability to attend practices or tournaments because of outside scheduling conflicts or lack of interest.
