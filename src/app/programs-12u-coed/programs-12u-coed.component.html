12U COED: Competition Team Program

Pumas has redesigned our 12U program, it is now COED, and more development based than tournament based. While focusing on tournaments and gearing practices for those tournaments are still a good way to learn the sport, we feel that this change has some big advantages.

  • Being skill and player focused will relieve some stress of learning a new sport. Focusing on the fun and skill of the game rather than the result of a tournament, lets the players progress quicker.
  • Players at this age should not be specializing at any one position. Players will learn and get to play every position during practices.
  • Having less time commitment initially allows players and their families an easier transition in to the commitment of youth sports, and most importantly, be enjoyed more by the players. At this age they should be experiencing multiple sports for better athletic development and enjoyment. This can be tough if one sport is monopolizing all of that Childs’ time.
  • Lower cost for just starting. Committing to a full competitive team is a large family commitment in both time and money. Each club has their own price according to their costs (courts, coaches, uniforms, etc..). For those that are just starting out paying over $1000 for a program, plus travel and hotel fees, can be enough to make the sport unapproachable for many families.
  • We can still play tournaments. While we will not be basing the practices on wins and losses, playing another team can be fun. If there is enough interest in playing tournaments, we will bring a team to play. There is no commitment to do any tournaments. The tournaments would be at no additional cost (other than team shorts).

Age Requirement

All players who meet the USAV requirement for 12U play are invited to attend our COED Program. Players must be under 12 through August 31st of the upcoming season year. Use the Age Group Tester to see if you're eligible for the 12U program. This program is recommended for ages 8 to 12. If younger than 8 years old, entry into the program would be on an individual basis after an assessment by the club director.

What is the experience required?

No previous volleyball or athletic experience necessary. Our instructional programs are designed with beginners in mind but all levels of play would benefit. Those players having experience above a beginner level can still benefit from repetitions through skill progressions and technique adjustments. We adjust our instruction based on the skill level of our participants. All players are accepted, regardless of background in the sport. Players learn at their own pace.

Adaptive Program

The 12U program uses a lighter ball and lower net. This is huge for children of this age not to have to play on the same size net or use the same size ball as adults. This helps the players progress in their development with a better self-image of what they can accomplish and what they dare to dream to accomplish. Triple Ball Based

Players will be learning all the volleyball skills for each positions. We will base the learning on triple-ball based tournament play. This effectively triples the amount of touches that the players have during a typical game. Practices will be based on getting them to love the sport, have fun while working hard, and prepare them for entry into the higher age group competitive teams. Triple-ball was started in Canada and is played by their 14 and under teams.

COED

In 2019-2020 the region has initiated 12U Triple ball as the first COED division While the genders are separated in most sports from 14 and older; learning volleyball, a non-contact sport, is highly effective in coed settings with ages 12 and under. This is just like their school. Unless your child goes to an all-Boys or all-Girls school their classes, including gym, are coed.

Benefits to COED play
  • At a young age, athletic skill matters more than gender. Coed play allows boys and girls to join a group based on ability rather than solely on gender. This promotes a great level of competition and increases skills.
  • Placing boys and girls together on one team also goes a long way in combatting gender stereotypes and preconceived ideas about girls in sports. Coed teams create a great social environment for kids and can counter the notion that females can't be as competitive as their male counterparts.
  • Girls start to grow faster at a younger age, keeping the level of competition equal.
  • Boys and girls can learn from each other. Having both genders on one team can change how the game is played for the better. Girls tend to be more pass-oriented, while boys usually focus more on their own success in a game (basketball based but relevant to volleyball). Coed teams can help both sides grow and adapt, giving children a wider set of skills as they move forward in their preferred sport.

What equipment does my child have to bring?

Any athletic attire is ok – t-shirt, shorts, athletic shoes. For shoes we do recommend volleyball specific shoes or basketball shoes as they are built for more of the stop and go, side to side motion of the game. Running shoes are built differently and lead to a higher risk factor for ankle injuries. Kneepads are recommended but not required. Any brand of soft knee pads available at your local sports store is acceptable. Please make sure your child has ample hydration (water or sports drinks are ok) at each session.

What are some of the skills focused on?

Hand-eye coordination, Motor skill development, Passing/defense, platform and footwork, Overhand passing/setting fundamentals, serving basics and foundation, attacking technique and strategy, defense and digging, serve receive, serving strategy.

What are some of the benefits?

Beside the progression and motor development of athletic sports skills players also learn that you can have fun while working hard, how to be a good teammate, what it means to commit to a team, your teammates and ultimately yourself.
