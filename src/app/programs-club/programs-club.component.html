Club Division

The Club Division has the largest participation from the Pumas Volleyball Club. About 83% of the girls play in this division. These teams will now practice twice per week; Once on Saturday mornings or afternoons for 2-1/2 hours (sometimes Sunday in an emergency) and once on Tuesdays evenings for 2 hours. Teams are formed by age, skill level demonstrated at tryouts, and the athletes' previous experience with Pumas. No teams are formed in advance of tryouts.

The Club season runs from October and November tryouts through the first weekend in May for most teams. Some teams may play through the end of May. Practices start in December and continue almost every Saturday and Tuesday through the end of the season. There are five to six Sunday single day tournaments and two to three multi-day tournament in the New England area. Club coaches may decide to participate in one to two other additional multi-day tournaments with that cost being paid by the team. The extra tournaments are not included in the season fee, and are voluntary. Parents provide transportation to practices and tournaments. Most Club tournaments are within an hour's drive, but some may be up to 2.5 hours away. Players should try to plan their vacations around practices and tournaments to the extent possible. During registration, players will be asked to indicate their tournament availability. Your availability and scheduling commitment level can affect what team you are placed onto.

Time Commitment Expectations

Players are expected to attend all practices and tournaments

Homework, school projects, and studying for tests are all excellent activities for your academic careers but are rarely intrusive upon JO volleyball tournaments and practices if you are planning and scheduling your time properly. Completing your academic work is the priority and our schedules are set up months in advance to allow you to do that. Learning to balance your time to get all of your academic work done should be taken very seriously. A coach should not find out the morning of a practice or tournament that you will not be able to attend. If you have a conflict with a tournament please notify your coach at least 30 days in advance. If you have a conflict with a practice, please notify your coach at least 24 hours before practice. Your coaches are planning your practice ahead of time knowing who will be attending. Failure to notify your coach can have a negative effect on the whole teams practice session.

If injured, you are still expected to be at practice

Of course, we would never ask you to sit at the gym if it proved to be detrimental to your recovery, but we would ask that you still consider your commitment to the team and to the club. There are many things to be learned by sitting on the sidelines listening or helping assist a coach while recovering from an injury.

If you are on a school sports team

We understand the desire to play as many sports as possible. We have had many multi-sport athletes in the past and have a few recommendations that will make it easier for you to balance your schedule or make those tough decisions about what to play. Playing multiple sports in one season requires:

  • A greater vigilance in balancing your schedule.
  • It is important that you be in touch with your high School coach (and let Pumas Volleyball Club know) so they understand you are competing in more than one sport.
  • Know all your sports schedules for practices and competitions before the season starts. This way you can research if you have major conflicts that will prohibit you from competing in either sport.
